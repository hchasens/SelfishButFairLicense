# Selfish But Fair License (SBFL) v1.0

> *"I want to share my work with the world — but I also want to eat."*

The **Selfish But Fair License (SBFL)** is a source-available software license designed for independent developers and small teams who want to build openly and collaboratively, without giving away their right to make a living from their work.

---

## Why Does This License Exist?

Most software licenses force you to choose between two extremes:

- **Fully open source** — anyone can use your work for anything, including building a competing business on top of it.
- **Fully proprietary** — nobody can see, learn from, or contribute to your code.

Neither felt right. The SBFL exists for the middle ground: developers who want their software to be open, contributable, and freely usable by the community — but who also want to reserve the right to commercialise their own work without someone else profiting from it without permission.

It's not greedy. It's just fair.

## Who Is This For?

The SBFL is a good fit if you are:

- An independent developer who wants community contributions but also plans to offer the software as a paid service.
- A small team building in the open who want protection against larger companies commercialising your work without compensation.
- Someone who believes open collaboration and personal sustainability aren't mutually exclusive.

## How to Apply This License to Your Project

1. Copy the `SBFL-v1.0-LICENSE.txt` file into the root of your repository.
2. Rename `SBFL-v1.0-LICENSE.txt` to `LICENSE.txt`.
3. Replace `[YEAR]` with the current year.
3. Add the following notice to your `README.md` and optionally to the top of your source files:

```
Copyright (c) [YEAR] the Original Author
Licensed under the Selfish But Fair License (SBFL) v1.0
See LICENSE file for full terms.
Commercial use requires a separate written license from the Original Author.
```
